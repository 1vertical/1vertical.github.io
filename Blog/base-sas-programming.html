Introduction

As someone with many interests, I find data analysis the most intriguing. Data is literally everywhere. Data analysis is one of the most useful subjects that you can master and applies to nearly all industries today with an inordinate amount of bits and bytes flinged, squeezed and pulsed around the world. But, what exactly does it entail? The next paragraphs will cover it all.

Why you should use data analytics

  • It helps with reducing costs
  • It assists with better decision making
  • It implements improved services

What is data analytics exactly?

This is the general process to data analysis...

  • Raw and exsiting data is inserted.
  • Raw data is then processed or analysed by analysts, this entails storing, organising and "mining" the data.
  • Resulting processed data provides insight, clarification and decisions to be made based on the data.

Raw data is facts that doesn't make sense on its own and isn't usable (due to various formats, and other reasons). Therefore, needs processing.

Storing and organising data means that the data is stored in a clear way to make sense of it.

Mining the data means to make use of various algorithms to make even more sense of the data.

TL;DR of what is data analytics?
Raw data IN that is unusable, PROCESS it to make it understandable then USE the sensible data to get insight and make better decisions.

What data analytical tools are available?

There are several in fact, included but not limited to:

  • SAS (We will focus on this one here)
  • R
  • Python
  • Apache Spark
  • Excel
  • Hive
  • Pig
  • ...

Odd names, huh? Tried and tested over the course of their existence and packs a punch. The pros and cons will be covered in a later post but for this article, we'll be focus on the one that is great for big pharma - SAS. And no, we are not talking about the special ops kind...

Why should you use SAS?

  • It is easy to learn
  • It supports graphical capabilities to visualize data
  • Its employability is king
  • It has advanced built-in toolsets and algorithms

What is SAS exactly?

SAS stands for Statistical Analytics System. It contains of a statistical package that has its own proprietary database management system (DBMS) and own programming language that is, compared to other languages, very straight forward and easy to use. Primary used in the health and financial industry to perform statistical analytics to solve problems and make better decisions.
